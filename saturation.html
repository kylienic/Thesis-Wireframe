Saturation

Saturation is color intensity. The simplest way to increase saturation is to convert the pixel data to HSB (Hue, Saturation, Brightness) and then adjust the saturation level, which then becomes simple linear arithmetic. Within RGB color space, adjusting the saturation is equivalent to increasing the difference between the color components. For example, if you have an RGB value of (200, 190, 201), you can increase the saturation by changing it to (200, 160, 220).

As a professional portrait photographer, I don't often adjust saturation outright. I may adjust the saturation of the reds in an image, for example, to decrease red in skin tone. Saturation tends to be a harsh adjustment that is more of a stylistic choice than anything else. Some Instagram filters adjust contrast quite obviously. Saturation is important in images where color is important, such as nature photography. Complete desaturation is the equivalent to converting an image to greyscale.

To increase saturation, increase the difference between RGB pixel components.

Increase: Larger difference between R, G, and B

Decrease: R, G, and B more similar (grey = completely identical RGB)
