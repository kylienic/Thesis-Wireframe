Proper exposure is necessary to express picture information. If an image is too dark (or underexposed), it can be brightened to bring out details in darker regions. If an image is overexposed, its brightness can be decreased, though the overexposure may have already resulted in a loss of pixel data. A desirable histogram is centered, without too much concentrated on the left (darker values) or the right (brighter values).

Qualitatively, brighter images tend to connote positive emotions due to their association with light. Darker images, due to the absence of light, connote darker emotions. The manipulation of brightness can impact the emotion of the photograph. Commercial photographs tend to be evenly lit, bright and well-exposed. More dramatic theatrical shots may be stylistically darker.

Mathematically, brightness is simple pixel addition. In order to brightnen an image, simply add any integer value to each of the pixels. To increase the brightness by 10, add 10 to each pixel.
